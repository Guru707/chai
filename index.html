The Journey of a Sales Person to a Chaiwala

Wondering how a sales executive takes a u-turn and opens a Chai shop?

The pandemic has been a life-changing experience for most of us. I lost my comfortable, 9 to 5, 6 digit salary job in a matter of a day. Immediately I started looking for a new job, hoping my degree and work experience would get me a job in no time. However, I ran out of luck with getting only a few responses, and those who did revert were offering jobs that were not good enough. I was home for 3 months bored at home, I decided to change the scene and went to my nearest Chai Tapri because I got bored with my home Tea. There I saw the chaiwala not following the guideline precautionary measures to make the tea, which concerned me. There I got my idea, serving Chai with hygiene. After doing research, the challenge is there are too many big giants currently serving tea but their menu is not affordable to all. I came across two challenges. First, how to make this brand affordable so that everyone can drink Chai - from Rickshaw drivers to the Luxury car owners. And the second challenge was how to make it more hygienic.

To keep pocket-friendly Hygiene, it was crucial to open a proper Chai outlet, not a Stall/Tapri, and to target the Rickshaw Driver to the Luxury Car Owner, quality must be maintained at Rs. 10/-. The new normal life is concentrated towards the hygiene factor as well as affordable chai for all. Keeping that in mind Chai Patram came into conception, making untouched chai that was hygienic, and affordable for all.
